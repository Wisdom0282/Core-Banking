01 ‚Äî Market Context

The emerging market opportunity no one designed for

Sub-Saharan Africa processes over $800 billion in inter-bank settlements annually, yet most of the dashboards treasury teams rely on were designed a decade ago ‚Äî built for Western market conditions, slow networks, and desktop-only workflows.

In markets like Nigeria, Ghana, and Kenya, treasury operators face a unique set of constraints: intermittent connectivity, mobile-first teams, extreme transaction volume spikes (particularly around month-end payroll cycles), and regulatory frameworks that demand real-time audit trails.

02 ‚Äî Problem Definition

Four critical pain points destroying treasury efficiency

Through 3 weeks of discovery ‚Äî including 14 contextual interviews with treasury analysts, operations managers, and compliance officers across Lagos, Accra, and Nairobi ‚Äî four systemic pain points emerged.

Critical ¬∑ UX Failure
No real-time failure alerting

Settlement failures surfaced only in batch reports, often 15‚Äì30 minutes after the event. Operators had no live alert mechanism ‚Äî manual log-checking was standard practice.

Critical ¬∑ Data Architecture
Liquidity data siloed by bank node

Treasury teams had to open 4‚Äì6 separate system screens to build a consolidated view of inter-bank positions. No single source of truth existed for real-time liquidity.

High ¬∑ Cognitive Load
Raw data with no contextual priority

Transaction feeds showed all entries equally ‚Äî a ‚Ç¶500M RTGS payment and a ‚Ç¶5,000 transfer displayed with identical visual weight. Operators missed high-value anomalies regularly.

High ¬∑ Accessibility
Desktop-only, no mobile access

Senior treasury managers who approve urgent transactions frequently work from mobile. The existing system was entirely unusable on phone screens, creating dangerous approval delays.

03 ‚Äî Design Process

From chaos to clarity in 16 weeks

01
Discovery & Contextual Research (Weeks 1‚Äì3)

14 interviews across 3 markets. Shadowed 4 treasury operators during live sessions ‚Äî one during a month-end payroll cycle with 11,000+ transactions. Mapped current-state workflows with a service blueprint. Identified 23 distinct pain points, prioritised to 4 critical.

02
Problem Framing & Design Principles (Weeks 3‚Äì4)

Defined 3 core design principles: (1) Urgency before volume ‚Äî critical alerts always dominate; (2) Position at a glance ‚Äî total liquidity readable in under 5 seconds; (3) Mobile-first decisions ‚Äî every approval action works on a 5" screen.

03
Information Architecture & Low-Fidelity (Weeks 5‚Äì7)

Ran 3 rounds of card sorting with 8 operators to establish data hierarchy. Built paper prototypes for the liquidity overview screen. Tested 4 different alert system models ‚Äî a persistent alert rail outperformed modal dialogs by 3x in task speed tests.

04
High-Fidelity Design & Usability Testing (Weeks 8‚Äì13)

2 rounds of usability testing with 6 participants each. Measured time-to-detect for simulated failure scenarios. Iterated on the settlement heatmap after participants misread colour scales. Designed a lightweight mobile view for decision-maker approval flows.

05
Pilot Launch & Measurement (Weeks 14‚Äì16)

Phased rollout across 3 banks. Instrumented with session analytics to track time-to-detect failures, alert response rates, and screen navigation patterns. Baseline metrics captured in week 1; comparative data at week 4 and 8 post-launch.

04 ‚Äî Solution Highlights

Three design decisions that changed everything

Design Decision 01
Alert Rail, not modals

A persistent left-rail showing critical failures and threshold breaches ‚Äî always visible regardless of scroll position. Operators no longer miss alerts while reviewing transaction feeds. Colour-coded severity (red ‚Üí amber ‚Üí blue) provides immediate triage without reading copy.

Design Decision 02
Position-first KPI header

Four headline metrics (total liquidity, settled volume, queue depth, failure count) anchor every screen. Designed to answer "are we healthy right now?" in under 5 seconds. Traffic-light colour logic removes the need to interpret numbers for senior stakeholders.

Design Decision 03
Amount-weighted transaction feed

High-value transactions surface with larger visual weight. A ‚Ç¶1B RTGS transfer occupies more visual real estate than routine low-value entries. Operators can scan for anomalies in the feed without reading every row ‚Äî a behaviour change validated in testing.

Design Decision 04
Responsive mobile approval mode

A stripped-down mobile view surfaces only pending approvals and critical alerts for decision-maker access. Swipe-to-approve with biometric confirmation reduced urgent transaction approval time from 8+ minutes (finding a desktop) to under 60 seconds.

06 ‚Äî Reflections

What I'd do differently ‚Äî and what I'd double down on

Biggest win
Observational research beats surveys in operational contexts

Shadow sessions during live trading hours revealed pain points no interview could surface ‚Äî the operator who had two browser tabs open side-by-side as a manual workaround told us more about the system's failure than 10 surveys.

What I'd change
Invest more in onboarding UX

Week 2 post-launch saw confusion with the new alert rail among staff who'd used the old system for 5+ years. A more structured progressive disclosure and onboarding flow would have reduced the change management burden significantly.

Key principle
In high-stakes interfaces, simplicity is a safety feature

Every design decision we made to reduce cognitive load ‚Äî fewer screens, stronger visual hierarchy, persistent alerts ‚Äî directly translated into fewer errors and faster response times. Complexity isn't power. Clarity is.
